About

So… what do you actually do?

I get asked this constantly. And after years of building ML systems, I still don't have a clean, dinner-party answer.

Here's the closest I've got:

I build models that make decisions in places where being wrong is expensive — and I spend most of my time making sure they're wrong as rarely, and as cheaply, as possible.

People picture the job as training something brilliant. The reality is stranger. The model is the easy 10%. The other 90% is everything around it.

Figuring out what the data is quietly hiding. Knowing when a result is real, and when it's just luck wearing a costume. Designing the system so that when the model fails — and it always eventually does — nothing important breaks with it.

The skill nobody warns you about isn't building intelligence. It's refusing to trust it until it's earned the right.

That's the part I find genuinely fascinating — and it's the part that never survives into the polished demo. So I'm going to start sharing more of it here. The real texture of building AI for high-stakes work. Failures included. Especially the failures.

Capability

What "end to end" actually means

Six stages, one owner. This is the lifecycle I run on every project — each stage has explicit gates, artifacts and accountability.

01

Framing & scoping

Translate a business goal into a measurable ML problem — success criteria, constraints, and a delivery plan the team can execute.

02

Data & pipelines

Ingestion and processing for high-frequency and multi-modal data: clean, point-in-time correct, and audit-ready from day one.

03

Feature engineering

Regime-aware feature families designed with domain rationale — not just statistical fit — and tracked through idea-to-decision reports.

04

Modeling & labeling

Deep learning, GANs and LLMs on carefully constructed labels. Baselines first; complexity only where it demonstrably pays.

05

Validation & governance

Look-ahead and leakage checks, pre-run input gates, and per-feature decision reports — everything earns its place with evidence.

06

Deployment & health

Ship low-latency, then monitor decay and regime mismatch with composite health scores. Keep, retrain, or retire — on data, not opinion.
